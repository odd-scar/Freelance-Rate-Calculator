How to price freelance work in 2026

The fastest way to undercharge is to start with what feels reasonable instead of what your business actually needs to survive. Use this framework to move from income goal to client-ready quote.

Step 1

Start with your annual target, not an hourly guess

Your rate needs to cover personal income, business overhead, taxes, and a profit cushion. If you back into pricing from those inputs, you stop treating every project like a gamble.

Step 2

Cut your available time in half first

Freelancers rarely bill forty hours a week. Sales, admin, revisions, scope management, and delivery all consume time, so your billable hours should be conservative.

Step 3

Turn hours into a fixed-fee offer

Clients prefer clear investment ranges. Once you know your sustainable rate, wrap it into a project quote with outcome-based language and a timeline premium for rush work.

Step 4

Monetize the next operational need

After a freelancer sets pricing, the next tasks are invoicing, bookkeeping, and getting paid. That is why SoloRate pairs educational content with partner tools.
